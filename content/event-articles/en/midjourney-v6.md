Midjourney version numbers are the community’s calendar. After V6 shipped in December 2023, the first act was often plain: run the same prompt side by side. Differences often showed up in fine instructions and short in-image text—a model that once won on mood began executing briefs more literally.

For designers, that matters more than “prettier.” Beauty can be hand-picked; listening lets a prompt become half a spec. Words on packaging, titles on posters, labels on boards move from unusable texture toward occasionally usable elements. Occasionally is already enough to change a workflow.

Midjourney still distributes through Discord and subscriptions, without open weights. Its historical seat is product and aesthetic defaults: countless tutorials, memes, and concept frames pass through its default look before other tools. V6 made that default more obedient and forced peers to catch up on text and instruction following.

Text-to-image progress is sometimes quiet. It only makes you delete fewer rejects and treats nouns in the prompt as more than decoration. V6’s sound is a slightly lighter trash bin of bad frames.
