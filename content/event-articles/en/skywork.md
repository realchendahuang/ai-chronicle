Tiangong and Skywork frequently appeared together, but they performed different jobs. Tiangong was the search-and-conversation product an ordinary user could open. Skywork was closer to a signature written for developers and the research community. For Kunlun, the dual branding was more than a naming exercise. A consumer interface by itself could be dismissed as a wrapper around somebody else’s API; a model repository by itself could not explain how the capability reached users.

The base-model Skywork project entered the open community in 2023. Its official repository described pretraining on 3.2 terabytes of data, primarily Chinese, English, and code, and released models alongside training data, evaluation data, and evaluation methods. By mid-2024, projects such as Skywork-MoE were documenting experience with mixture-of-experts training. Individual leaderboard positions would move. The open material had a more durable function: outsiders could inspect evidence that Kunlun was training and studying models rather than simply purchasing access and changing the label.

Tiangong placed that work at the other end of the chain in search-augmented conversation and multimodal products. A user encountered an interface for questions, research, and content generation without needing to know the repository name underneath. The product supplied distribution and usage feedback; the model organization supplied a recognizable technical identity. Together they formed Kunlun’s survival structure in the foundation-model market. The company did not need to lead every capability line to retain its own model name, customer relationship, and release cadence.

Later branches showed that the structure was not limited to chat. Skywork-o1 moved toward reasoning, SkyReels extended the organization into human-centered video generation, and additional multimodal and agent projects followed. Those later results should not be written backward as a finished matrix in mid-2024. They were consequences of the earlier choice. Once a research team, a model brand, and a product entrance remained in place, new branches had somewhere to attach.

Skywork records an alternative available to a second-tier internet company. Kunlun did not place its entire identity in one breakout chat application, nor did it rely on a single open release. Tiangong and Skywork separately guarded the entrance and the foundation. The structure was expensive and its roadmap could look untidy, but it left the company with some authority over which capability its products would absorb next in a market where model supply was becoming increasingly concentrated.
