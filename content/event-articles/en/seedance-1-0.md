On 11 June 2025, Seedance 1.0 entered the sentences of Volcano Engine’s Force conference. ByteDance did not lack shots; it lacked a video foundation name outsiders could point at—CapCut filters and ranking algorithms did not answer “where is your Sora.”

Seedance was described as supporting text/image input and multi-shot narrative, with a Pro tier; a tech report opened the same day. Numbers and ranks come from vendor and third-party setups and must be sourced. What enters the chronicle is distribution: Doubao, Jimeng, and cloud APIs put the model name on doors people open daily. The same matrix day also carried Doubao 1.6—video and language shared one stage.

When super-platforms do video generation, wins often sit in data and distribution, not single demos. Seedance pushed ByteDance from “effects that move inside a toolchain” into a column on the comparison sheet. Later 2.0 copyright storms would remind everyone: the more generations look like finished film, the closer law and platform liability draw. 1.0’s seat is naming and doorway; the bill for the name arrives later.
