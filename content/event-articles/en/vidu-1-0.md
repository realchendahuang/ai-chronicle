In late April 2024, when Shengshu pushed Vidu into public trials, global video generation was still ruled by Sora demo frames. The demos were beautiful; the door was narrow. Vidu’s move was plainer: register, prompt, wait, download—turning “China has text-to-video too” from a headline into a click that could fail.

It stressed temporal consistency and character stability. Those words need product boundaries: longer shots still artifact; complex subjects still drift. Historical seat is not zero artifacts but timing—before Kling’s mass productization and Runway Gen-3, a startup had already taken a cell with a queueable product.

For creators, Chinese prompts and local content habits lowered first-kilometer friction. For the industry, comparison sheets could no longer list only OpenAI and Runway. Later Vidu versions would change quality and control, but 1.0’s meaning is the door pushed open: how long the corridor is depends on whether each generation still dares to let ordinary people click in.
