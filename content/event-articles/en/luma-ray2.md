On 15 January 2025, Luma swapped Ray2 into Dream Machine. For subscribers this was not a paper TOC update but a new default in a dropdown: same prompt, more coherent motion, events that feel more like “what happens next.”

Official talk mentioned roughly 10× prior training compute, 5- or 9-second clips, and higher-resolution options. Compute multiples are vendor language; user proof remains reject rates. Ray2’s product intent is clear: after speed dividends, sell “runs naturally.”

It crowded the same season as Runway Gen-3, Kling, and soon-productized Sora. Tool-company calendars are rewritten by competitor keynotes. Ray2 did not end artifacts; it wrote “is motion believable” as a paid point. Creators still pay time—and the willingness to believe the next regenerate will be better.
