For a long stretch, Chinese video-generation stories happened inside apps: upload a prompt, wait in a queue, download a clip, never see weights. Around December 2024, Tencent Hunyuan placed another path on GitHub and model hubs—HunyuanVideo and related components as open weights at a scale local teams had to take seriously.

The dissonance is habit. People know capability seeping through WeChat better than the Tencent name beside reproducible train and serve scripts. Open video needs more than a pretty demo; it needs pipelines, evals, and docs outsiders can run. By opening, Hunyuan accepted that ecosystem seat can also be built on Hugging Face download curves.

Later Avatar, image-to-video, and 1.5 refreshes show this was not a one-off PR beat. A parallel open 3D line shows Hunyuan trying to hand in homework across generative media modalities at once. For researchers and startups the meaning is practical: fewer backbones trained from zero, more business logic on giant checkpoints.

When a git clone log prints a giant’s model name, the power structure of video generation loosens a little—from apply-for-beta toward fork.
