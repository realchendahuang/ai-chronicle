The keyword at WWDC 2024 was not a chatbot name. Apple called it Apple Intelligence: writing tools, notification summaries, image play, a Siri that could use personal context—all on a system feature list. Models were described as on-device systems coordinated with Private Cloud Compute, not “please open our app.”

The wording is a stance. For a year, consumer generative AI’s default door was a dialogue box. Apple arrived late yet held the screen people unlock daily. It chose to make intelligence a button inside Mail and Notes, and to make privacy sentences part of the keynote: how personal context is handled, whether it trains ads.

Public technical talk mentioned roughly 3B-parameter on-device models and adapters. The number is modest; the intent is not: to show small models under system permissions and hardware acceleration can become default experience. Features rolling by region and language remind readers that announcement is not simultaneous global possession.

When Settings matter more than a chat icon, consumer AI gains another battlefield—the operating system. Android and PC on-device stories afterward all answer this page of notes.
