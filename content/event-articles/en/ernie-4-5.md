On 16 March 2025, two new names appeared in ERNIE Bot’s model selector. ERNIE 4.5 targeted native multimodal understanding; ERNIE X1 emphasized “deep thinking.” Both entered the consumer product and Baidu AI Cloud’s Qianfan platform, and both were made free for ordinary users. The models became clickable options without waiting for the end of a large keynote.

The two names carried different product questions. ERNIE 4.5 was meant to handle inputs beyond text, bringing images and other modalities into a flagship connected to Baidu’s search and content systems. X1 answered the reasoning-model wave by giving users a separate path for tasks that might benefit from a longer inference process. Baidu did not present one system as universally sufficient. It divided “see more” and “think longer” into two routes, then placed them behind the same doorway.

Free access was the release’s most concrete distribution move. After DeepSeek-R1, Chinese users had begun comparing models with a new combination of reasoning ability, response time, and price. Baidu already controlled search traffic, an ERNIE user base, and the Qianfan enterprise platform. It could move new models into many existing accounts without waiting for developers to adopt another application. For a consumer, the change was a dropdown selection. For an enterprise, model identifiers, price, latency, and output behavior all returned to the integration checklist.

Baidu positioned 4.5 around multimodal capability and X1 around reasoning cost-performance. Those remained publisher claims. Free trials could create a large testing population, but did not demonstrate equal reliability on every task. Longer reasoning could also increase latency and resource use. Meaningful comparison required submitting the same documents, images, code, and business questions to both models, then observing where each failed—not merely preserving the rankings on a launch page.

The company also announced a plan to open the 4.5 family later. That promise and the free product available that day were two different deliveries. One had appeared in chat and cloud interfaces; the other would count only when weights, licensing, and runnable documentation actually arrived. Placing both in one strategy showed how strongly open-weight ecosystems were influencing Baidu. On a timeline, however, the promise still belonged in the future tense.

The release of ERNIE 4.5 and X1 ultimately demonstrated a large platform’s ability to distribute quickly. Baidu did not answer the new competition with a technical report alone. It made multimodality and reasoning into two buttons that existing users could test on the same day. Access itself remained a form of model power: laboratories could train new systems, while platform companies could determine how many people would actually use them the following morning.
