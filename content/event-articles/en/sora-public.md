In February 2024, Sora was a demo. Around 9 December 2024, it became a pressable generation control inside entitled ChatGPT Plus/Pro accounts. In those ten months Kling, Vidu, and Runway had filled the blank of “waiting for OpenAI” with products. Productization is not coronation; it is buying the ticket late.

Regions, quotas, and safety policy draw real borders. Users do not receive an infinite world simulator but constrained short-video generation counts. World-simulator rhetoric remains; billing and queues pull it back to software.

Historical seats must be split: demos change expectation; products change clicks. Both matter; they are not the same day. Later Sora lines may still rename, shrink, or relaunch—the chronicle should record the first jump to “generatable for subscribers,” not pretend the terminus is fixed.
