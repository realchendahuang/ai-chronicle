In summer 2024, a concrete thing happened in many local text-to-image workflows: the default checkpoint shifted from SDXL to files bearing the word Flux. No balloon drop—only Discord and GitHub links, and a tiered license: Pro on the API, Dev as research weights, Schnell for people who needed speed.

Inside the field, Black Forest Labs barely needed introduction: core authors on the Stable Diffusion path had founded a new house. SDXL had already pushed open aesthetics high, yet still often yielded to closed services on complex prompts and on-image text. Flux’s pull was a fresh attack on those obedience details.

Within weeks, ComfyUI nodes, hosts, and tutorials migrated. Not because a theorem updated, but because the same workflow produced fewer rejects and closer prompt matches. Licenses must stay explicit: not every weight equals freely commercial open source. Open and semi-open side by side is the product grammar of this era.

In text-to-image history, brands are sometimes stickier than architectures. Flux recalls that default aesthetics are often moved by small groups who train and release again and again. When they change the doorplate, the community’s default path changes too.
