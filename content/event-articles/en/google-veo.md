At I/O in May 2024, Google threw Veo. Three months after the Sora demo, video generation could no longer belong to one OpenAI blog. DeepMind’s name stood on the main stage with 1080p and longer-clip claims.

Availability still came in stages: VideoFX, cloud, and Gemini channels opened gradually. Historical seat is naming and pairing—the sheet must keep a column for Google. Kling and Runway filled products the same year; Veo filled the platform-lab flagship empty cell.

Search and cloud distribution imaginations followed: if video generation grows inside Gemini, the door is not a waitlist but already-logged-in accounts at scale. That is later; I/O day plants the flag first.
