At HDC on 21 June 2024, Huawei Cloud pushed Pangu 5.0 forward: NLP, multimodal, vision, prediction, scientific computing, sizes from billion-scale up. It felt less like a chatbot keynote than an industrial catalog—which tier, on what compute, for which industry.

Under constrained compute, the catalog itself is a stance: models must deploy on obtainable hardware, not only live on someone else’s cloud price list. Ascend, cloud, and industry templates share one narrative. Capability demos can be pretty; the chronicle should remember constraints.

Pangu’s seat is therefore not a single leaderboard crown but a full-stack junction. Later open moves and disputes will open other chapters; 5.0’s day records this: one axis of the domestic foundation race is “can you deliver on your own silicon.”
