summaryEn: The European Union published the AI Act in its Official Journal, establishing tiered rules for prohibited uses, high-risk systems, transparency, and general-purpose AI models.
background: 各国此前主要依靠隐私、消费者保护和行业法规处理 AI。生成式模型快速普及后，模型提供者、部署者和下游产品的责任边界更加紧迫。
backgroundEn: Countries had previously relied on privacy, consumer-protection, and sector rules for AI. Rapid adoption of generative models made the duties of providers, deployers, and downstream products more urgent.
whatHappened: 法案禁止部分不可接受用途，对医疗、就业、教育等高风险系统规定数据、记录和监督义务，并为通用模型及系统性风险模型设置额外要求。条款分阶段生效。
whatHappenedEn: The law prohibited certain unacceptable uses, imposed data, record-keeping, and oversight duties on high-risk systems, and added obligations for general-purpose and systemic-risk models. Provisions entered into force in stages.
whyImportant: 它把 AI 治理从自愿原则推进到可执行法律，并产生类似 GDPR 的域外影响。企业必须把风险分类、文档和评估纳入产品开发流程。
whyImportantEn: It moved AI governance from voluntary principles to enforceable law and created extraterritorial effects similar to GDPR. Companies had to bring risk classification, documentation, and evaluation into product development.
impact:
  developer: 高风险系统需要可追溯数据、技术文档、日志和人工监督设计。
  industry: 模型和应用进入欧洲市场需要按角色和风险承担不同合规义务。
impactEn:
  developer: High-risk systems require traceable data, technical documentation, logs, and human oversight.
  industry: Models and applications entering Europe carry different compliance duties by role and risk.
beforeAfter:

beforeAfterEn:
  before: AI governance relied mainly on principles, sector rules, and case-by-case enforcement.
  after: Comprehensive risk classes and general-purpose model duties entered statute.
