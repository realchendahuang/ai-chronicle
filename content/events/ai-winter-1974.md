summary: 1973 年 Lighthill 报告否定英国 AI 研究的实际进展，叠加机器翻译和感知机项目失利，英美公共资金随后明显收缩。
summaryEn: The 1973 Lighthill report rejected the practical progress of British AI research. Together with failures in machine translation and perceptrons, it contributed to a sharp contraction in UK and US public funding.
whatHappened: 早期系统只能在规模很小、规则明确的环境中工作，换到真实问题便遭遇组合爆炸。英国政府在报告后撤掉多数 AI 资助；美国 DARPA 也收紧目标和预算。不同国家与项目的降温时间并不完全一致，“第一次 AI 寒冬”是后来对这段收缩的概括。
whatHappenedEn: Early systems worked only in small, well-defined settings and ran into combinatorial explosion on real problems. The UK withdrew most AI funding after the report, while DARPA tightened goals and budgets. The cooling occurred at different times across countries and programs; “the first AI winter” is a later summary of the contraction.
whyImportant: 资金下降并非因为智能问题已被证明无解，而是研究承诺、评估尺度和实际能力严重错位。此后每一轮 AI 热潮都重复面对同一个问题：演示能否在更大范围内稳定工作。
whyImportantEn: Funding did not fall because intelligence had been proved impossible. Promises, evaluation scale, and actual capability had drifted too far apart. Every later AI boom faced the same question of whether a demonstration would remain reliable at larger scale.
impact:
  industry: AI 研究资金大幅缩减，许多实验室关闭。
